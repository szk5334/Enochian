1.2 The Four Components of a Working

Every working in this system uses four dyads: Host (H), Elemental (E), Initial Graft (I), and Terminal Graft (T). Each serves a distinct function.

The Host Dyad (H)

The Host provides the stable foundation and governing context for the entire working. It contributes the Calvary Cross (the spine and arms of the Subangle) to the newly grafted structure. Think of the Host as the container or environment in which the transformation occurs.

The Host determines:

  • The meta-environment governing the operation
  • Which Watchtower's hierarchy (God Names, King, Seniors, etc.) you invoke
  • The stable architecture around which the grafts are assembled

The Host does not change during the working. It is the constant context that holds and governs whatever transformation the grafts produce.

The Initial Graft (I)

The Initial Graft provides the starting point of the transformation. It contributes columns to the newly grafted Subangle based on where the first two letters of the Servient Angel are located. Think of the Initial Graft as "where you are coming from" or "what you are working with."

The Initial Graft determines:

  • The beginning state of the alchemical transformation
  • The material or condition being acted upon
  • The first half of the directional motion (I in the formula)

The Initial Graft represents the raw material, the problem, or the current state that the working will transform.

The Terminal Graft (T)

The Terminal Graft provides the end point of the transformation. It contributes columns to the newly grafted Subangle based on where the last two letters of the Servient Angel are located. Think of the Terminal Graft as "where you are going" or "what you are becoming."

The Terminal Graft determines:

  • The achieved state of the alchemical transformation
  • The goal or outcome being manifested
  • The second half of the directional motion (T in the formula)

The Terminal Graft represents the destination, the solution, or the transformed state that the working produces.

The Elemental Dyad (E)

The Elemental Dyad, realized through the Servient Angel, provides the operational mode and method of the working. It determines HOW the transformation propagates, not WHAT transforms. Think of the Elemental Dyad as the medium or channel through which the alchemical forces operate.

The Elemental Dyad determines:

  • The mode of operation (mental, emotional, structural, or active)
  • The method by which change manifests
  • Which specific Servient Angel carries the working

The Elemental Dyad remains constant throughout the operation. While the alchemical forces (Host and Grafts) may describe transformation, the Elemental Dyad describes the sustained style of that transformation.

1.3 Understanding Dyads

A dyad is written as Outer --> Inner (or equivalently, Inner within Outer).

Outer = the dominant CONTEXT, container, or governing force
Inner = the emerging INTENTION, content, or nascent force

Example: White --> Red means "within a purifying context (White), sovereignty (Red) is emerging."

Because each alchemical color and each element is itself a force-in-motion, a dyad represents a VECTOR - directional transformation with specific properties. Reversing the positions (Red --> White instead of White --> Red) creates a fundamentally different operation.

1.4 The Alchemical Colors

The four alchemical colors represent phases of transformation. Each is a force-in-motion, not a static quality.

Red in the Macro (Watchtower) Context

Red at the Watchtower level governs workings of embodiment, sovereignty, and manifestation. When Red is the Macro color, the entire working operates within a context of claiming authority, establishing presence, and bringing things into full expression. Red Watchtower workings tend toward assertive, commanding operations where the practitioner exercises sovereign will.

Use Red Macro for: Establishing authority, claiming territory, manifesting intentions, completing processes, exercising sovereignty, taking command.

Black in the Macro (Watchtower) Context

Black at the Watchtower level governs workings of dissolution, breakdown, and shadow confrontation. When Black is the Macro color, the entire working operates within a context of entropy, clearing, and reducing to fundamentals. Black Watchtower workings tend toward operations that break down existing structures, reveal hidden foundations, or clear away what no longer serves.

Use Black Macro for: Dissolving obstacles, breaking patterns, shadow work, clearing accumulated debris, stripping away illusions, returning to basics.

White in the Macro (Watchtower) Context

White at the Watchtower level governs workings of purification, refinement, and clarification. When White is the Macro color, the entire working operates within a context of cleansing, distillation, and increasing coherence. White Watchtower workings tend toward operations that remove impurities, bring clarity, and separate essence from dross.

Use White Macro for: Purification, cleansing, gaining clarity, refining processes, filtering influences, achieving precision, distilling essence.

Green in the Macro (Watchtower) Context

Green at the Watchtower level governs workings of growth, generation, and organic expansion. When Green is the Macro color, the entire working operates within a context of proliferation, connection, and vital development. Green Watchtower workings tend toward operations that create, multiply, network, and foster living systems.

Use Green Macro for: Growth, creation, expansion, networking, fertility, nurturing development, building connections, fostering abundance.

Red in the Micro (Subangle) Context

Red at the Subangle level specifies the emerging intention within the broader Watchtower context. As the Micro color, Red represents sovereignty, authority, or manifestation that is crystallizing within whatever larger context governs. Red Micro adds a quality of completion, claiming, or embodiment to the working.

Use Red Micro for: Adding sovereign completion, manifesting within a process, claiming authority within a context, bringing something to full expression.

Black in the Micro (Subangle) Context

Black at the Subangle level specifies dissolution or breakdown as the emerging intention. As the Micro color, Black represents entropy, clearing, or shadow exposure crystallizing within the broader context. Black Micro adds a quality of breaking down, revealing foundations, or confronting shadow.

Use Black Micro for: Adding dissolution within a process, breaking down specific elements, revealing hidden aspects, clearing within a larger context.

White in the Micro (Subangle) Context

White at the Subangle level specifies purification or refinement as the emerging intention. As the Micro color, White represents cleansing, clarity, or distillation crystallizing within the broader context. White Micro adds a quality of purifying, clarifying, or filtering specific elements.

Use White Micro for: Adding purification within a process, clarifying specific aspects, filtering particular influences, refining within context.

Green in the Micro (Subangle) Context

Green at the Subangle level specifies growth or generation as the emerging intention. As the Micro color, Green represents proliferation, connection, or organic development crystallizing within the broader context. Green Micro adds a quality of growing, expanding, or nurturing specific elements.

Use Green Micro for: Adding growth within a process, expanding specific aspects, creating connections, nurturing development within a larger context.

1.5 The Elemental Modes

The four elements describe HOW change propagates, not WHAT changes. They are the operational method through which alchemical forces manifest.

Air in the Macro (Row) Context

Air as the Macro element means the operation primarily moves through the mental realm - through thought, communication, understanding, and strategy. Air Macro operations work by changing how things are conceived, planned, communicated, or understood. The working propagates through ideas and words.

Use Air Macro for: Mental operations, communication work, strategic planning, intellectual development, clarity-seeking, establishing mental boundaries.

Water in the Macro (Row) Context

Water as the Macro element means the operation primarily moves through the emotional realm - through feeling, intuition, receptivity, and flow. Water Macro operations work by changing how things are felt, sensed, or emotionally processed. The working propagates through empathy and adaptation.

Use Water Macro for: Emotional processing, relationship work, intuitive development, adaptive responses, flow states, establishing emotional boundaries.

Earth in the Macro (Row) Context

Earth as the Macro element means the operation primarily moves through the structural realm - through building, anchoring, materializing, and stabilizing. Earth Macro operations work by changing tangible structures, physical reality, or stable foundations. The working propagates through solid manifestation.

Use Earth Macro for: Manifestation work, building tangible things, grounding operations, structural establishment, material world engagement, physical boundaries.

Fire in the Macro (Row) Context

Fire as the Macro element means the operation primarily moves through the active realm - through will, direct action, transformation, and intensity. Fire Macro operations work by forcing change through energetic intervention. The working propagates through dynamic force and catalytic action.

Use Fire Macro for: Crisis intervention, rapid change, willful transformation, dynamic action, catalytic work, establishing energetic boundaries.

Air in the Micro (Column) Context

Air as the Micro element specifies the emerging operational mode within the broader elemental context. Air Micro adds mental clarity, communicative precision, or strategic thinking to whatever primary mode governs the working.

Use Air Micro for: Adding mental precision, clarifying communication, strategic refinement within the primary operational mode.

Water in the Micro (Column) Context

Water as the Micro element specifies emotional flow or intuitive sensitivity emerging within the broader operational context. Water Micro adds empathic responsiveness, adaptive flexibility, or emotional depth to the working.

Use Water Micro for: Adding emotional sensitivity, intuitive guidance, adaptive flexibility within the primary operational mode.

Earth in the Micro (Column) Context

Earth as the Micro element specifies grounding or structural anchoring emerging within the broader operational context. Earth Micro adds stability, tangible results, or practical manifestation to the working.

Use Earth Micro for: Adding structural stability, grounding results, practical anchoring within the primary operational mode.

Fire in the Micro (Column) Context

Fire as the Micro element specifies dynamic action or willful force emerging within the broader operational context. Fire Micro adds urgency, intensity, or catalytic transformation to the working.

Use Fire Micro for: Adding dynamic force, urgent action, catalytic intensity within the primary operational mode.

Many recipes include alternatives, marked with letter suffixes (a, b, c). These are NOT interchangeable—each serves a different situation.

Appendix A: Configuration Types

Understanding the five configuration types is essential for designing your own workings. Configuration type determines how your working behaves over time, how self-maintaining it is, and how difficult it will be to reverse.

What Configuration Type Means

Configuration type describes the RELATIONSHIP between three alchemical dyads: H = Host (the governing meta-environment) I = Initial Graft (the starting state) T = Terminal Graft (the ending state) The Elemental Dyad (E) does NOT participate in configuration type. It defines HOW the work happens, but configuration type is determined solely by the relationships among H, I, and T. There are exactly five possible relationship patterns: Type I: All three are different (H ≠ I ≠ T) Type II: Host matches start (H = I, both differ from T) Type III: Host matches end (H = T, both differ from I) Type IV: All three match (H = I = T) Type V: Grafts match, host differs (H differs from I = T)

Type I: Linear Transition

Structure: H ≠ I ≠ T (all different)

Mechanical Properties: • Pattern is NOT self-reinforcing • Host provides neutral or contrasting environment • Working requires some active maintenance to persist • Natural decay occurs when reinforcement ceases • Maximum flexibility in execution and reversal What This Creates: The transformation happens under external governance. The environment shapes but does not lock the change. Think of it as working in a rented space. When to Use: • Exploratory work where you want flexibility • Learning arcs where the outcome may need adjustment • Healing processes that should complete and release • Any working where you want the pattern to fade naturally Reversal: Easiest. Simply ceasing to reinforce often allows natural decay.

Type II: Origin Reinforcement

Structure: H = I, both differ from T

Mechanical Properties: • Starting state is amplified by host reinforcement • Departure from established ground has momentum • Creates "launching pad" effect • Moderate persistence (stronger than Type I) • The beginning is home; the ending is the journey What This Creates: Your current state or existing strength becomes the foundation from which transformation launches. Think of it as building an addition onto your house. When to Use: • Expansion from existing strength • Extending established patterns or authority • Building on what you already have • Scaling existing sovereignty or capability Reversal: Moderate difficulty. Returning to origin is natural since it's already law.

Type III: Destination Locking

Structure: H = T, both differ from I

Mechanical Properties: • Achieved state becomes self-maintaining • Creates recursive stabilization • High persistence (self-reinforcing) • Natural decay is insufficient for reversal • The destination is baked into reality What This Creates: The ending state does not merely happen—it becomes the new ambient reality that enforces itself. Think of the destination having gravity that holds you there. Special Subtype: Mirror Inversion When I and T are exact reversals (X→Y becoming Y→X) and H = T, you create a self-correcting feedback loop. Extremely powerful for protection and sovereignty. When to Use: • Sovereignty installation • Protection workings that should maintain themselves • Identity-level shifts that should persist • Creating new baseline states Reversal: Difficult. Must break host-graft unity through active intervention.

Type IV: Absolute Identity

Structure: H = I = T (all same)

Mechanical Properties: • Total fixation, no motion permitted • Self-reinforcing from all angles • Change is structurally prevented • Maximum persistence (requires external force to break) • Natural decay is impossible What This Creates: A sealed state where change cannot occur. Reality is locked into this pattern until external force is applied to break it. Critical Consideration: Type IV cannot adapt. What you lock stays locked. Use only when you genuinely need permanence, and always have a reversal strategy prepared. When to Use: • Absolute boundaries (protection from severe abuse) • Seals and wards on bounded spaces • Complete severance of bonds • When adaptation would be harmful Reversal: Most difficult. No internal leverage—must apply external force.

Type V: Parallel Reinforcement

Structure: H differs from I = T

Mechanical Properties: • Grafts maintain themselves against host pressure • Not self-locking (requires active maintenance) • Creates definition through opposition • Moderate persistence • Forge-tempering logic: pressure creates resilience What This Creates: A state that holds steady despite environmental opposition. Think of standing firm in wind—stable through effort, not fixation. When to Use: • Maintaining states in hostile environments • Holding vision despite opposition • Creative flow in rigid contexts • Discipline under temptation • Integrity work Reversal: Moderate. Remove opposition pressure or redirect with supportive host.

Appendix B: Class Structure and Selection

Every dyad belongs to one of four classes based on the relationship between its Outer and Inner components. Understanding class structure enables you to select dyads that match your intent and control the intensity of your workings.

The Canonical Ordering

Class structure is based on a canonical sequence: ALCHEMICAL: Black (1) → White (2) → Green (3) → Red (4) → [Black] ELEMENTAL: Air (1) → Water (2) → Earth (3) → Fire (4) → [Air] These orderings are STRUCTURAL, not prescriptive. They establish class relationships but do NOT mandate progression order. Any transition is valid.

Class 0: Identity

Inner = Outer (same force on both sides)

Alchemical Members: Black→Black (pure dissolution), White→White (pure purification) Green→Green (pure growth), Red→Red (pure sovereignty) Elemental Members: Air→Air (pure mentality), Water→Water (pure emotionality) Earth→Earth (pure grounding), Fire→Fire (pure action) Semantic Property: Self-reinforcing stasis, fixation, no internal motion What It Creates: • Reinforcement of a single force • Maximum intensity of that one quality • Resistance to change When to Use: Seals, wards, maximum intensity, anchoring states Risk: Compounds fixation when multiple Class 0 dyads combine

Class +1: Progressive

Inner is +1 position forward in canonical sequence

Alchemical Members: Black→White (dissolution enabling purification) White→Green (purification enabling growth) Green→Red (growth enabling manifestation) Red→Black (sovereignty enabling dissolution) Elemental Members: Air→Water (thought flowing to emotion) Water→Earth (emotion grounding to structure) Earth→Fire (structure igniting to action) Fire→Air (action clarifying to thought) Semantic Property: Developmental flow, organic progression, natural next step What It Creates: Forward motion, natural development, momentum building When to Use: Developmental processes, gradual change, learning arcs, skill building Risk: Generally safe. Low risk profile. Can feel slow if breakthrough needed.

Class -1: Regressive

Inner is -1 position backward in canonical sequence

Alchemical Members: White→Black (purification through dissolution) Green→White (growth through purification) Red→Green (sovereignty through growth) Black→Red (dissolution through sovereignty) Elemental Members: Air→Fire (thought returning to action) Water→Air (emotion returning to clarity) Earth→Water (structure returning to flow) Fire→Earth (action returning to grounding) Semantic Property: Corrective motion, return, grounding, conservation What It Creates: Return to previous phase, grounding, conservation of essence When to Use: Error correction, grounding, integration, returning to fundamentals Risk: Generally safe and stabilizing. Provides grounding for complex work.

Class +/-2: Opposition

Inner skips one position (opposite side of the wheel)

Alchemical Members: Black→Green (dissolution bridging to growth) White→Red (purification bridging to sovereignty) Green→Black (growth bridging to dissolution) Red→White (sovereignty bridging to purification) Elemental Members: Air→Earth (thought bridging to grounding) Water→Fire (emotion bridging to action) Earth→Air (grounding bridging to thought) Fire→Water (action bridging to emotion) Semantic Property: Dialectical tension, skip-level bridging, productive paradox What It Creates: Skip-level motion, integration of opposites, quantum leaps When to Use: Breakthrough workings, integrating opposites, sophisticated work Risk: Higher complexity. Appendix D: Permanence, Fixation, and Risk

This appendix addresses critical considerations around workings that persist, lock, or become difficult to reverse. Understanding these dynamics enables informed choice rather than accidental entrapment.

The Permanence Spectrum

What Creates Fixation

Fixation occurs when a pattern becomes self-reinforcing and resists change. TYPE IV CONFIGURATIONS: When H = I = T, everything reinforces everything else. No internal leverage point for change. Structurally sealed. CLASS 0 DYADS: Identity dyads (X→X) create single-force reinforcement with no motion. Multiple Class 0 dyads compound the fixation. TYPE III WITHOUT EXIT VECTORS: When H = T and the achieved state has no natural development path, the destination locks without flexibility. THE CORE TRADEOFF: Fixation trades ADAPTABILITY for STABILITY. Appropriate when: • The threat is severe and ongoing • The commitment should be absolute • The scope is bounded (a space, a relationship, a pattern) NOT appropriate when: • Circumstances may change • Growth and development are needed • The scope is broad (identity, life direction)

Risk Assessment Model

Risk in this system is ADVISORY, not PROHIBITIVE. The system describes what configurations do; you decide whether those effects serve your purpose. LOW RISK: Type I configurations Class +1 and Class -1 dyads Exit vectors present Natural decay possible MEDIUM RISK: Type II and Type V configurations Sustained single-class patterns Some fixation potential Moderate reversal effort HIGH RISK: Type III configurations (especially without Mirror Inversion) Type IV configurations Class 0 dyads in critical positions No exit vectors, difficult reversal EXTREME RISK: Type IV with Class 0 dyads Black→Black fixation (perpetual dissolution) Systemic configurations affecting identity Multiple compounding fixation factors

Appendix E: Designing Reversals

The ability to undo work is essential to practitioner sovereignty. Every working should be deployed with understanding of how to reverse it.

Foundational Principles

AXIOM 1: ALL WORKINGS CAN BE UNDONE Nothing is truly permanent unless you lack knowledge to reverse it. Irreversibility is a choice, not a fate. AXIOM 2: UNDOING IS NOT "UN-HAPPENING" The effects occurred and shaped reality. Reversal means: • Releasing the enforced pattern • Allowing natural motion to resume • Removing artificial constraints Analogy: Undoing is like removing a dam, not reversing the river. AXIOM 3: EASE OF REVERSAL VARIES BY CONFIGURATION Type I: Easiest — pattern is not self-reinforcing Type II: Easy to moderate — return to established origin Type V: Moderate — remove opposition pressure Type III: Difficult — must break host-graft unity Type IV: Most difficult — no internal leverage, requires external force AXIOM 4: TIME AFFECTS REVERSAL COMPLEXITY Recent workings: Easier (pattern not deeply established) Established workings: Harder (reality adapted around them) AXIOM 5: SOME REVERSALS REQUIRE INTERMEDIATE STEPS Complex configurations may need sequential operations to safely unwind.

The Three Reversal Approaches

DISMANTLE: Active Deconstruction Deploy a new working specifically designed to undo the previous. Properties: Fastest, highest practitioner involvement When to Use: Type III and IV, when speed matters, when complete removal needed Design: Use forces opposing the locked pattern. Black often necessary. TRANSFORM: Redirect Rather Than Undo Deploy a working that supersedes the previous, making original obsolete. Properties: Medium involvement, creates new state rather than return When to Use: When new direction more useful than return, when direct confrontation would cause whiplash Design: New pattern must be strong enough to supersede. DECAY: Allow Natural Dissolution Stop reinforcing the working and let it fade naturally. Properties: Slowest, lowest involvement, gentlest transition When to Use: Type I only, Type II if new origin established elsewhere CRITICAL: Does NOT work for Type III, Type IV, or any self-reinforcing pattern

Reversal by Configuration Type

REVERSING TYPE I: Strategy: DECAY usually sufficient. Stop maintaining. If active reversal needed: Simple negation or redirect with new Type I REVERSING TYPE II: Strategy: Return to origin (already established) or establish new origin Configuration: Type II with T matching desired return state REVERSING TYPE III: Strategy: Must DISMANTLE or TRANSFORM. Decay will not work. Key: Separate Host from T via new Host, dissolution, or superseding Type III Special Case: Mirror Inversion requires disrupting the feedback mechanism itself REVERSING TYPE IV: Strategy: Apply EXTERNAL FORCE. Internal leverage does not exist. Breaking: Black forces applied from outside; may need sustained effort Multi-Phase: Weaken seal → create crack → establish alternative → supersede REVERSING TYPE V: Strategy: Remove opposition pressure or redirect with supportive host Without pressure to resist, Type V often releases naturally
